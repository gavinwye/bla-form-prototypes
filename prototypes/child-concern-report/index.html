Report a concern about a child

Last updated: 26 March 2026

Use this service to report a concern about a child who may be at risk of abuse, neglect, or harm. Your report will be sent to the Child Care Board of Barbados for assessment and investigation.

You do not need to be certain that a child is being abused or neglected. If you have a concern, it is important to report it so that it can be assessed by a professional.

If a child is in immediate danger

Call the Royal Barbados Police Force on 211 or the Child Care Board emergency line on (246) 435-8999.

How to report

Complete the online form below. It takes approximately 10 to 15 minutes.

You can report anonymously. However, providing your contact details allows the Child Care Board to follow up with you if they need more information.

Complete the online form

What you will need to share

You will be asked to provide as much of the following information as you can:

  • Your name and contact details (optional)
  • Your relationship to the child
  • The child's name, age, and gender
  • The child's address or location
  • The nature of your concern
  • Details of what you have seen, heard, or been told
  • Whether the child is in immediate danger
  • Details of any other people or agencies involved

Other ways to report

You can also report a concern by:

  • Calling the Child Care Board on (246) 436-8934
  • Visiting the Child Care Board at Fred Edghill Building, Cheapside Road, St. Michael
